What is Energy?

Energy is the capacity to do work or cause change, existing in various forms such as kinetic, potential, thermal, electrical, chemical, and nuclear energy. It is a fundamental concept in physics and plays a vital role in all physical interactions, biological functions, and technological applications.

Energy exists in several forms: kinetic (motion), potential (stored), thermal (heat), chemical (bonds), electrical (charges), and nuclear (atomic nucleus). The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.

Types of Energy

There are two types of energies based on their source strength:

  • Non‑Renewable Energy
